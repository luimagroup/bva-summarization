The veteran's service medical records confirm the motor vehicle accident (MVA).
Service connection for PTSD, in particular, requires: (1) medical evidence diagnosing the condition in accordance with 38 C.F.R. ยง 4.125(a) (i.e., DSM-IV); (2) credible supporting evidence that the claimed in-service stressor(s) occurred; and (3) medical evidence establishing a link between the current diagnosis and the stressor(s) in service.
He denied efforts to avoid thoughts, feeling or conversations associated with the MVA as well as efforts to avoid activities, places or people that aroused recollection of the event.
Overall, the Board finds that the evidence shows that the veteran did not incur or aggravate PTSD during active duty.
